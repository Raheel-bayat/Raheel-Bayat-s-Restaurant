Ingredients
  • Chicken: 800 grams, cleaned and cut into pieces
  • Basmati Rice: 2 cups, soaked for 30 minutes
  • Yogurt: 2 tablespoons
  • Onions: 2 large, thinly sliced
  • Tomatoes: 1 cup, chopped
  • Ginger-Garlic Paste: 3 teaspoons
  • Spices: Turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, garam masala, cumin seeds, bay leaves, cinnamon, cardamom, cloves
  • Mint and Coriander Leaves: Fresh, for garnish
  • Saffron Milk: Optional, for aroma and color
  • Oil or Ghee: For cooking
Instructions
  1. Marinate the Chicken: Mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Coat the chicken pieces and let them marinate for at least 1 hour.
  2. Prepare the Rice: Boil water with bay leaves, cinnamon, cardamom, cloves, and salt. Cook the soaked rice until 70% done, then drain.
  3. Cook the Chicken: Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Fry onions until golden brown. Add tomatoes, ginger-garlic paste, and the marinated chicken. Cook until the chicken is tender and the oil separates.
  4. Layering: In a large pot, layer the partially cooked rice and chicken alternately. Sprinkle mint and coriander leaves, fried onions, and saffron milk between layers.
  5. Dum Cooking: Cover the pot with a tight lid and cook on low heat for 20-30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  6. Rest and Serve: Let the biriyani rest for 10 minutes before serving. Pair it with a simple salad.
